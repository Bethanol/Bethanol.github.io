Base Stats

The Basics

Before we can get to stats there are a few ground rules that we need to go over first. The first thing we need to go over is who and what the dungeon master, or DM for short, does. The DM is essencially the game master. They control the world around you. They control things like npcs, enemies, allies, and the setting around you. Their role is to set up and immerce you into the game. They also control how well your character does at something. This is done by stat, ability, and saving throw rolls. All of these rolls are rolled with a 20 sided dice (d20). The higher rolls you get on this dice the more likely you are to succeed at what you try. Whenever you do roll some of these things you will usually get a modifier which is based on which stat you are rolling. This is explained in further detail bellow.

The 6 Main Stats

These stats make up the backbone of character creation. These 6 basic stats are as follows: Strength (Str), Dexterity (Dex), Constitution (Con), Intelligence (Int), Wisdom (Wis), and Charisma (Cha). Each one of them represents and affects their own seperate things. In this section we will be focusing on what they do and their modifiers.

Stat Modifiers

As said before, modifiers are numbers that can change your roll. When you roll for certain stats or abilities you will use these certain modifiers. For example, if the dm asks me to roll a Str check, I will roll and add my Str modifier to the roll. These modifiers are determined by the number you put into your stat. Now these numbers have restrictions. Your stats cannot go below 1 or above 20. If one of your stats reach 0, you die. For every even number your modifier will increase by one. Strength (Str)

Str is a pretty straight forward stat. This pertains to how strong a character is. It is mainly effects carrying capacity, attack rolls for Str based weapons, and feats of strength. For example, if you wanted to throw something extremely heavy, say like a boulder, at an enemy you would first have to roll a strength check to see if you can pick the item up and then roll again to see if you can successfully throw it. Now your carrying capacity will be whatever your Str score is, multiplied by 15. So you'll at least be able to have a 15lb. carrying capacity.

Dexterity (Dex)

Dex pertains to how nimble and flexable your character is. This stat influences your armor class, or AC for short, attack rolls with Dex based weapons, how acrobatic you are, and initiative. Now before we continue AC is a number that determines what rolls can and can't hit you. Now just like everything else attack rolls are made with a d20. You will also get modifiers on your attack rolls that are based on what weapon you are using. The weapon you choose to attack with will determine what stat you will roll to hit with it as well as if you get you get your proficiency modifier added to it, more on that later. Anyway, when an attack roll gets rolled against you your AC defines the lowest number needed on the roll to hit you. So for example, if your AC is a 13 than an enemy needs a 13 or above on an attack roll to hit you. Now when it comes to calculating AC it will normally depend on what armor you wear. Below is a table that shows all the different types of armor and how they're used to calculate AC. If you are not wearing armor, AC will usually be calculated by just adding 10 to your Dex modifier. For the unusual circumstances go to the Classes tab and look under Monk and Barbarian. Furthermore, intiative is a roll soley based on Dex as well. Intiative is used to determine turn order during battle. This number is equal to your Dex modifier. So the higher your Dex the more likely you will get to go first in combat. Not only that, but Dex can also be used to dodge, climb, and parkour. For example, if you want to jump off a high structure you would role Dex to see how well you land.

Constitution (Con)

Con refers to your character's endurance. This stat only effects a character's health and a few class abilities. It is the only stat in the game that doesn't have any abilities linked to it. Con rolls will only be used to protect against things like poison and pain. It's basicaly a measure of your character's well-being. To calculate your characters health will depend on both your class and your con stat. Therefore, I will be going over that aspect of the game in the classes tab.

Intelligence (Int)

Int is another self explainatory stat. This stat pertains to how knowledgable your character is. It is basically book smarts in a nutshell. It can also effect spell effectiveness depending on which class you choose, more on that in the classes tab. Int will mainly be used to remember history, learn about a spell, and to look for clues.

Wisdom (Wis)

Just like Int is book smarts, Wis is street smarts. It portrays how good your character is at figuring things out. How to basically notice and sus things out. This stat will be used to tell if someone is lying, sus something out that others might not notice, and even to treat wounds.

Charisma (Cha)

Finaly, Cha is how presentable you make yourself out to be. It's your power to sway or coerce people to your side of an argument. The stat is used for scaring, lying, convincing, and entertaining players and npcs alike. It's how well you can put up a facade, or bullshit your way out of a situation, or even sing for an audience.

Applying Stat Values

So now that we understand what each stat does how do we give values to these stats. Obviously you can't just give each stat whatever value you want or else you could break the game. In turn, there are 3 allocation methods that the game provides. There is standard array, point-buy, and rolled. Below I go into each method in depth.

Standard Array

Standard array gives you 6 values for each stat. These values are static, which basically means when you create a character these are the values you get. You can only use each value once, so once you assaign it to a stat you can't assign the same number to a different stat. So then what are these static numbers? The values are as follows: 15, 14, 13, 12, 10, and 8. This means you will always get two +2's, two +1s, a +0, and a -1. Below is an example on how to use standard array.

Point-buy

Point-buy is an allocation method that allows you add points to specific stats. The way this works is that each stat value starts at 8. You then have a total of 27 points you can add to different stats. There are limitations to this assignment method. You have a maximum and a minimum number your stats can be. So, your stat cannot go over 15 or go below 8. Furthermore, it takes an extra point to get to 14 and 15. This means you will have to spend 2 points in order to make your stats higher than a 13. This then brings the max amount of points you can spend to 9 and a min of 0.

Rolled

Rolled stats are exactly as they sound. They are stats that you have to roll to aquire. Now there are multiple homebrew rules for leaving it less up to chance. More on that in the hombrew tab. Anyway, the way rolled stats works are as follows. First you need 4 d6s. You will roll these all at the same time. Then, when all 4 are rolled, take the lowest number you rolled and put it aside. Then add the other 3. This total will be the stat value you can assaign. You will do this 6 times for all 6 stats. Once all of your stats are rolled you can then put them into your desired stat.
