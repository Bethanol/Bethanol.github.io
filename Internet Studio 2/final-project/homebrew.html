D&D as a Whole

Now what I think is the greatest thing about D&D is that no one plays it the same way. That's right some of these rules that I took so long to disscuss might not even be used. But that's up to your dm. Your dm makes the game, the experiance, the characters, and the world. Now because this is a factor I can't teach you everything there is to know about D&D. So instead I will teach my experiances with these homebrew rules that I have encountered.

Base Stat Rolling

Now as I have explained how the base stats, abilities, and saving throws all worked with each other, but how do we get those numbers in the first place. There are a few ways to get these stats however the one that I have experianced the most is rolling for stats. The way this works is that you use 4d6's and roll them all at the same time. Then you take the lowest number out and then add the other 3 rolls together as shown below. However I have had many dms that don't like to leave it too much to chance. I've had a dm who always replaced your 2nd lowest stat with an 18, a dm who said I could re-roll if I got below a 10, and even had a dm that alowed me to re-roll 1s that I got on the d6's.

Overpowered Class Aspects

Another thing great dms do is game balance. Now the game itself is already pretty balanced, Wizards of The Coast made well sure of that. But sometimes the rules can be too vague. If a player is abusing something that really shouldn't be abused, then of course you need to balance the game. For example, one of the things that I ran into was an exploit. I was playing a rogue and a rogue has something called flanking. What the rules said was basically if an ally was within 5 feet of me and attacking the same enemy then I could proc sneak attack. I won't lie I abused this fact. Until finaly the dm had to put his foot down and banned flanking from our campaign. And now I have never had a dm since then that allowed flanking.

Homebrew Classes

Homebrew can be one of the best things in the world. Especially if you colaborate your plans with your dm. Many dms love making homebrew items, weapons, races, and classes. Even I did this once. My group and I were all prepping for a D&D one-shot in our dm's universe. I was under the impression that this one-shot was going to be absolute bat shit insane. So I decided to go bat shit on my character creation. Now I won't lie I have no idea how I got my dm to agree this, but he and I collaborated together to make, and I shit you not, a piss gun. It was fully operational with napalm, sleep, and berserk rounds. Colaborate with your dms, you might be suprised.

Homebrew Worlds

When I first started D&D with my friends we were just playing one of the easiest modules to begin with. But after that, we immeadiatly jumped into homebrew. Ever since then my friends and I have prospered in the many worlds my dms have thrown us in. The fights, the roleplay, the fun it is so undeniably intoxicating. These worlds that your dms build for fun, it's not for nothing.
