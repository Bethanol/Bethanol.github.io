Races and Their Importance

Each race in the game is rather important when it comes to class choice. Each race has their own special aspects, shaped around what they are. Many races are better at one class than others are. Now before we continue this, I have to define another rule of the game. So some races get something called features or feats for short. These feats can also be gained by leveling up your class as well. These feats will usually enhance a modifier, give an extra proficiency, or give the player an extra action, bonus action, or reaction. If you want to dive deeper into feats click this link here ( all feats available). Furthermore, for more information on actions go to the Classes tab. This makes choosing a race very important. It won't only be what you'll look like, but how you will play like as well. In this section I will be going over all racial aspects. This includes all racial atributes, physical characteristics, and what classes benefit from these aspects. However, I will only be going over the races in the players handbook. This is because these races you will guarenteed play, races from any other source book are up to your DM's discretion. If you want to go further down the rabit hole of the playable D&D races, click this link ( all playable races) in order to peruse the massive database.
